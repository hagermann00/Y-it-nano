## PAGES 10-11: MODULE 4 - THE 8 FAILURE POINTS

### PAGE 10: THE CASCADE BEGINS

**[VISUAL: 8 boxes cascading down like falling dominoes]**

**FAILURE POINT 1: CAPITAL LOCK** (Month 0-1)
You start with $2,000. You spend $500 on Shopify setup, course, samples.
$1,500 left. You buy inventory: $1,500 worth of products tied up.
You can't touch it for 2-3 months. Your cash is completely frozen.
If you need emergency money? You're stuck.
Cost of failure point: Loss of financial flexibility, forced to continue even if you know it won't work.

**FAILURE POINT 2: FEE SHOCK** (Month 1-2)
You made your first sale! $30 revenue!
Then you discover the fees:
- Shopify payment processing: 2.9% + $0.30 = $1.17
- PayPal processing: 2.9% + $0.30 = $1.17
- Refund request: -$30 (customer's money back)
Total: You keep $28 from $30
But you paid $8 for the product, shipped it ($3 to customer), FB ads ($15) = -$12.

That "$30 sale" cost you money.
Cost of failure point: Realizing 40-50% of your margins evaporate before you touch profits.

**FAILURE POINT 3: SLOW SALES** (Month 2-3)
Your ads are running: $400/month spent.
You've sold 5 items in the first month.
Conversion rate: 0.1% (10% of website visitors buy)
Industry standard: 1-3% conversion

At your rate: Break-even requires 3,600 more sales.
At 5 sales/month: That's 720 months = 60 years.

You panic and start "scaling ads" = spending more to fix what's broken.
Cost of failure point: Chasing losses, doubling down on broken strategy.

**FAILURE POINT 4: MARGIN COMPRESSION** (Month 3-4)
Your product: Phone stand you're selling for $24.99
Product cost: $6.50
Shipping: $2
Fees/returns: $4
Expected margin: $12 (48%)

Then 10 competitors enter the market selling the same product.
Price war begins.
You lower price to $19.99 to compete.
New margin: $7 (28%)

That's a 42% margin reduction.
To hit your $1,000 daily target, you now need 50% more sales.
Which requires 50% more ad spend.
Which requires a smaller profit margin.
Which requires even MORE sales.

This is the death spiral.
Cost of failure point: Impossible economics at scale.

---

### PAGE 11: THE CASCADE CONTINUES

**FAILURE POINT 5: INVENTORY TRAP** (Month 4-5)
You ordered 200 units of your "winning product."
Turns out, it's not as winning as you thought.
Sales slowed after Month 3 (competitor price war did that).
You have 70% of inventory unsold.
FBA storage fees: $150-300/month
They charge by cubic foot.
That $1,200 inventory investment now costs $200/month.
Every month it doesn't sell, it costs you 16.7% of its value.

After 6 months of storage fees: You've paid $1,200 + $900 in fees = $2,100 for inventory that would sell for $800.
Cost of failure point: Dead money that bleeds you dry.

**FAILURE POINT 6: POLICY SHOCK** (Month 5-6)
Amazon announces (without warning) that FBA fees are increasing 20%.
Your $12 profit becomes $9 profit.
Or Shopify changes its payment processing structure.
Or Facebook tightens ad policies and your account gets restricted.
These aren't hypothetical—they happen regularly.

When policies change, individual sellers can't negotiate.
Giant retailers absorb the hit. You close the door.
Cost of failure point: Complete business disruption beyond your control.

**FAILURE POINT 7: RETURN RATES SPIKE** (Month 5+)
Month 1 returns: 2%
Month 3 returns: 8%
Month 5 returns: 20-30%

Why?
- Shipping takes 30-60 days (customers forget what they ordered, don't want it anymore)
- Quality issues surface after use
- Sizing/fit problems (for apparel)
- Customers bought on impulse (they calm down after two weeks)

When returns hit 20%, your profit margin disappears.

Customer pays $24.99.
Shipping: -$2.50 (10%)
Processing: -$0.75 (3%)
Returns: -$5.00 (20%)
Net to you: $16

You keep $16. Product costs you $8. Net profit: $8.

But if return rate is 25%? You're losing money.

This is when people realize dropshipping doesn't work.

Cost of failure point: Profitability becomes impossible.

**FAILURE POINT 8: THE EXIT** (Month 6+)
By Month 6, most people quit.
They've tested 8-12 products.
They've spent $2,000-4,000.
They've made $500-1,000 in revenue.
Net loss: -$1,500 to -$3,500.
Time invested: 300+ hours.
Effective hourly rate: -$5 to -$12/hour.

They close the store, cancel the subscription, move on.
Some take another course (thinking they "didn't execute right").
Most just quit.

Cost of failure point: The business model itself is the problem.

[CALLOUT BOX - Large]
**This cascade is predictable.**
**It's not bad luck. It's not your fault.**
**It's the model.**

The research shows 90% of dropshippers follow this exact path. Month 1 excitement. Month 3 doubt. Month 6 exit.

The 10% who succeed? They had MORE capital, BETTER ads skills, or EXISTING audience.

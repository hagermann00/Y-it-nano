### CHART 1: The Survival Curve (100 → 2)

**Title:** "100 People Start FBA. 2 Remain Viable After 5 Years."

**Visual Concept:**
A descending curve rendered as gravitational collapse. 100 people at the top left represented as individual dots/pixels. As the curve descends (time axis: months 1-60), dots progressively disappear. Not abstractly—viscerally. The curve itself is rendered with texture showing where people quit: visible "ledges" where clusters exit simultaneously (month 3, month 6, month 12). The final 2 survivors are rendered in a different color, isolated at the far right. The aesthetic is geological: like watching erosion, like watching a cliff face crumble into the sea.

**Color Palette:**
- **Starting population:** Hopeful gold (#F59E0B) - 100 dots
- **Attrition zones:** Gradient gray (#9CA3AF → #374151)
- **Survivors:** Lonely gold (#C7930D) - 2 remaining dots
- **Baseline:** Black (#000000)
- **Grid:** Subtle gray (#E5E7EB)

**Data Points (Y-axis):**
```
Month 1: 85 people
Month 3: 50 people (first cliff edge)
Month 6: 20 people (second collapse)
Month 12: 5 people
Year 2: 2 people (stable)
```

**Emotional Directives:**
- Curve should feel like gravitational pull downward
- Ledges/plateaus show momentary stabilization before next drop
- Texture should suggest entropy, degradation, loss
- Final 2 dots should be isolated, almost unreachable
- Overall effect: watching possibility narrow to statistical impossibility

- **Color Space:** sRGB for digital, CMYK conversion available
- **File Size Target:** <500KB per portrait (PNG optimization)

- **Portrait Generation (Midjourney/DALL-E):** 1-2 iterations per character (~5 min × 7 = 35 min)
- **SVG Chart Creation:** 30-45 min per chart (4 charts = 2-3 hours)
- **Quality Review & Revision:** 30 min per asset

## SECTION 4: PRODUCTION NOTES & ARTIST GUIDANCE

### Tone & Aesthetic Philosophy

**Core Instruction:** These images should make the reader uncomfortable in an intellectually honest way. Not with emotional manipulation, but with structural clarity. The viewer should see a person and immediately understand that their failure isn't about them—it's about the system. That's the power of these images.

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Motivational aesthetics (no sunrise, no hope glows)
- ❌ Personal blame (not a "lazy person" narrative)
- ❌ Cartoonish exaggeration (these are serious documents)
- ❌ Generic stock photography (sterile, corporate)
- ❌ Overly artistic abstraction (lose the human element)

**Pursue:**
- ✓ Documentary realism (real light, real space, real emotion)
- ✓ Structural visualization (show the system, not the person)
- ✓ Color as emotion (grading should convey mood)

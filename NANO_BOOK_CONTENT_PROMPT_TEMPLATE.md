# Y-IT NANO BOOK CONTENT GENERATION PROMPT
## Universal Template for 24-Page Business Reality Books

---

## PROMPT STRUCTURE FOR AI CONTENT GENERATION

### SYSTEM CONTEXT

You are writing a Y-It nano book - a brutally honest, research-backed analysis of why a specific business opportunity fails for 85-95% of people who try it. Your mission is to expose the gap between guru promises and statistical reality through satirical analysis, real data, and compressed case studies.

**Brand Voice:** Satirical, contrarian, empirically-driven, darkly humorous without cruelty
**Target Audience:** People researching business opportunities who want truth over motivation
**Format:** 24-page, 6" × 9" KDP book with full-color interior
**Tone Arc:** Cynical/analytical (Modules 1-7) → Honest/constructive (Module 8)

## THE 8-MODULE STRUCTURE (Pages 4-19: 16 pages)

### MODULE 1: THE DREAM THEY'RE SELLING (Pages 4-5, Spread)

**Content Prompt:**
Write a two-page analysis exposing the gap between guru promises and reality.

**PAGE 4 (LEFT): The Promise**
- Header graphic (1" tall): "MODULE 1: The Dream"
- 3-5 representative guru quotes (real or composite, attributed)
- Bullet list of 6-8 common promises:
  * Startup cost claims ("Start with just $X")
  * Income promises ("Make $X/month in Y days")
  * Time commitment lies ("Just X hours/week")
  * Difficulty level ("Anyone can do this")
  * Competition myths ("Blue ocean opportunity")
  * Passive income claims ("Set and forget")
- Bottom illustration: starry eyes, dollar signs, hope imagery

**PAGE 5 (RIGHT): The Reality**
- Header graphic: "The Reality Meter"
- Visual bar chart showing promise vs. reality gap
- Data box with ACTUAL STATISTICS:
  * Success rate: [X]%
  * Median income: $[X]-$[X]/year
  * Time investment: [X]+ hours
  * Real startup cost: $[X]-$[X]
- Source citations (2-3 credible sources)
- Bottom illustration: deflating balloon or reality check visual

**Required Elements:**
- Minimum 3 specific statistics with sources
- Minimum 3 guru quotes (satirical but realistic)
- Clear visual contrast between promise and reality
- Word count: ~600-800 words total (both pages)

### MODULE 2: HOW IT'S SOLD (Pages 6-7, Spread)

**Content Prompt:**
Deconstruct the marketing funnel and emotional manipulation tactics used to sell this opportunity.

**PAGE 6 (LEFT): The Sales Funnel**
- Header: "How They Get You"
- 5-stage funnel breakdown:
  * Step 1: AWARENESS (social media ads, fear triggers)
  * Step 2: CONSIDERATION (free webinar, "secret system")
  * Step 3: DECISION (limited time offer, FOMO + social proof)
  * Step 4: PURCHASE (course delivered instantly)
  * Step 5: REALITY (months later, where's the success?)
- Visual funnel diagram with percentages at each stage
- Icon for each stage showing emotional state

**PAGE 7 (RIGHT): The Actual Timeline**
- Header: "What Actually Happens"
- Timeline graph showing motivation decay:
  * Month 0: "I'm going to do this!" - MOTIVATION: 100%
  * Month 2: "Why isn't this working?" - MOTIVATION: 50%
  * Month 4: "I've lost $X,000..." - MOTIVATION: 15%
  * Month 6: "I quit." - MOTIVATION: 0%
- Callout box: "90% of people quit between months 3-6"
- Bottom: Reality check icon (skull or warning symbol)

**Required Elements:**
- Specific marketing tactics named
- Emotional arc clearly mapped
- Timeline based on actual failure patterns
- Word count: ~600-800 words total (both pages)

### MODULE 4: FAILURE POINTS (Pages 10-11, Spread)

**Content Prompt:**
Map the predictable cascade of failures and the critical decision moment.

**PAGE 10 (LEFT): The Cascade**
- Header: "Where It Falls Apart"
- 6-stage failure cascade (visual boxes):
  * Month 1-2: CAPITAL SHOCK ("Wait, this costs more?")
  * Month 3-4: COMPETITION HITS ("There are 500 competitors?")
  * Month 5-6: MARGIN COLLAPSE ("I'm making $2/sale?")
  * Month 6-8: CUSTOMER CHURN ("Nobody buys from me?")
  * Month 8-10: BURNOUT ("I'm exhausted.")
  * Month 10-12: EXIT ("I quit.")
- Caption: "This pattern repeats across 95% of failures"

**PAGE 11 (RIGHT): The Critical Moment**
- Header: "The Point of No Return"
- Timeline showing decision points:
  * Week 1-2: Can still abort cheaply
  * Week 3-4: Capital committed (hard to back out)
  * Month 2: Sunk cost fallacy kicks in
  * Month 3-4: CRITICAL MOMENT (most people realize it won't work but guilt/hope keeps them going)
  * Month 6-9: Psychological breaking point (finally quit, too late to save capital)
- Callout: "Most people wait until month 6+ to quit but realize in month 3 that it won't work."
- Icon: Clock showing time passage

**Required Elements:**
- Business-specific failure points
- Realistic timeline based on research
- Psychological aspects of failure cascade
- Word count: ~600-800 words total (both pages)

## GENERATION PROMPT TEMPLATE

Use this prompt structure when generating content for a new Y-It nano book:

```
Create a 24-page Y-It nano book analyzing why [BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY] fails for 85-95% of people who try it.

BUSINESS TYPE: [Dropshipping / Print-on-Demand / Affiliate Marketing / etc.]

RESEARCH INPUT:
- Failure rate: [X]%
- Average time to failure: [X] months
- Average capital loss: $[X]-$[X]
- Median income: $[X]/year
- Top guru promises: [List 3-5 common claims]
- Hidden costs: [List 5-8 unexpected expenses]
- Main failure points: [List 3-5 common failure mechanisms]

CASE STUDY ARCHETYPES:
1. [Name] - [Archetype description] - [Failure summary]
2. [Name] - [Archetype description] - [Failure summary]
[Continue for 7-11 total]

Generate complete content following the 8-module structure:
- Module 1: The Dream They're Selling (guru promises vs. reality)
- Module 2: How It's Sold (marketing funnel deconstruction)
- Module 3: The Hidden Costs (complete financial reality)
- Module 4: Failure Points (predictable cascade)
- Module 5: Tales from the Trenches (compressed case studies)
- Module 6: The Numbers (statistical analysis)
- Module 7: The Verdict (decision framework + alternatives)
- Module 8: Call to Action (ecosystem connection)

Target: 7,800 words, satirical but credible tone, minimum 10 sourced statistics, 7-11 case studies.
```

## DESIGN SPECIFICATIONS

### Technical Specifications
- **Trim Size:** 6" × 9"
- **Page Count:** 24 pages
- **Color:** Full color interior (CMYK)
- **DPI:** 300 minimum
- **Margins:** Inside 0.75", Outside 0.5", Top/Bottom 0.5"
- **Bleed:** 0.125" all sides

### Typography
- **Headers:** Bebas Neue, 18-22pt, Y-It Red (#E63946)
- **Subheaders:** Open Sans Bold, 14-16pt
- **Body:** Lato, 11pt, Dark Gray (#333333)
- **Data/Callouts:** Source Sans Pro Bold, 12-14pt
- **Captions:** Lato Italic, 9-10pt

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Dark gray (#333333) for text
- **Accent 1:** Y-It red (#E63946) for headers
- **Accent 2:** Light gray (#F5F5F5) for backgrounds
- **Accent 3:** Gold (#D4AF37) for success/data boxes

### Visual Elements Required
1. Hero image (title page, 2/3 page)
2. Cost breakdown chart (waterfall or bar)
3. Timeline graphic (motivation decay)
4. Failure cascade diagram (6-stage boxes)
5. Survival curve graph (line chart)
6. Decision framework flowchart
7. Character portraits/avatars (7-11 case studies)
8. Data visualization boxes (3-5 throughout)

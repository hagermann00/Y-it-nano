## ARCHETYPE-SPECIFIC INSIGHTS

### Unique Contributions by Case Study

**CS 001 (Sarah Martinez - Side Hustler):**
- Highlights fashion niche oversaturation (30-40% return rates)
- Shows how professional skills (marketing) don't transfer to saturated commodity markets
- Demonstrates opportunity cost for high-earners ($7,200 foregone freelance income)

**CS 002 (Marcus Chen - Side Hustler):**
- Illustrates how technical/analytical skills ≠ marketing skills
- Facebook algorithm as "black box" for engineers
- Energy depletion after day job preventing optimal decision-making

**CS 003 (David Thompson - Unemployed):**
- Demonstrates why unemployment is WORST time to start dropshipping
- Desperation feedback loop destroying rational testing
- Severance as survival fund, not investment capital

**CS 004 (Jennifer Hayes - Unemployed):**
- Predatory "mom entrepreneur" marketing targeting vulnerable single mothers
- Employment gap costs exceeding dropshipping losses
- Borrowed money from parents creating family strain

**CS 005 (Tyler Rodriguez - College Student):**
- Shiny object syndrome pattern (dropshipping → FBA → crypto → NFTs)
- Student loan debt used for speculative ventures (catastrophic decision)
- "Entrepreneur" identity over actual execution

**CS 006 (Robert Kim - Career Transitioner):**
- Mid-life crisis vulnerability to "quit your 9-5" messaging
- Corporate buyers won't use unknown Shopify stores (Amazon dominance)
- Risk aversion at 42 preventing aggressive testing required for success

**CS 007 (Amanda Foster - Serial Entrepreneur):**
- 5-year pattern across 6 ventures losing $7,900 total
- Jack of all trades, master of none = poverty
- Completion beats exploration (most valuable insight)

**CS 008 (Lisa Patterson - Stay-at-Home Parent):**
- "Work from home with kids" is incompatible with dropshipping's focus requirements
- Time fragmentation (15-minute blocks vs. 2-hour focused sessions needed)
- Guilt spiral unique to parent archetype
- Missed moments with children ages 3 and 6 (irreplaceable)

**CS 009 (Jake Morrison - Influencer - SUCCESS EXCEPTION):**
- Shows even "success" ($10,240 profit) can be net-negative (-$26,160 opportunity cost)
- Audience as asset vs. resource to extract
- Platform algorithm punishment for promotional content
- Better monetization: Affiliate marketing vs. dropshipping operations

**CS 010 (Raj Patel - Immigrant):**
- Exceptional work ethic (90-hour weeks) cannot overcome systemic barriers
- $0.77/hour effective rate despite 520 hours effort
- Health costs (25 pounds weight gain, pre-diabetic) not calculated in financial loss
- Same work ethic applied to skills-based career = $110K income (validation of effort, not model)

**CS 011 (Patricia Williams - Retiree):**
- Technology learning curve 3-5× steeper for non-digital natives
- Facebook account ban = deletion of 8 years digital social life (catastrophic for retirees)
- "Never too late" marketing as elder exploitation
- Fixed income makes $2,000 loss feel like $10,000 loss to younger person

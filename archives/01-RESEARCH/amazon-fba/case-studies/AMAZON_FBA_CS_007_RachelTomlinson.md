# Case Study 007: Rachel Tomlinson

## Character Profile
- **Name:** Rachel Tomlinson (composite, anonymized)
- **Age:** 27
- **Background:** Social media manager, influencer network
- **Income:** $58,000/year
- **Why They Tried:** "I have an influencer audience of 45K followers. I can use that to launch an FBA product."

## Their Plan
- Sell aesthetic phone cases and screen protectors
- Target: 45K Instagram followers, 0.5% conversion = 225 units first month
- Initial investment: $2,200 for 500 units
- Strategy: Cross-promote with influencer network, build brand
- Timeline: "Build to 1K units/month by month 4 using social proof"

## What Happened

### Product Research Phase
Rachel researched "phone cases" using Helium 10 and JungleScout (popular FBA research tools). Search volume: 150,000/month. Market looked huge. Didn't analyze deeply: oversaturated categories often have high search volume because everyone is selling them.

She also didn't use the Facebook Ad Library to check competitor density. Had she done so, she would have seen 400+ active ads for phone cases from different sellers.

### Month 1
Launched to 45K followers with story promo. Expected 225 conversions. Actual: 18 sales from social. Reason: followers expected free/influencer discount, not full price. Built 500 units into FBA warehouse hoping organic Amazon traffic would help.

Organic Amazon traffic: terrible. Her product listing was #47 in a category with 12,000 listings. Zero visibility without paid ads.

### Month 1-2
Spent $800 on Amazon PPC (pay-per-click ads). Results: 45 sales, $1,050 revenue, $0.35 per click cost (high), click-through rate 0.4% (low). Her product wasn't compelling vs. competing 12,000 listings.

She competed against:
- Established brands with 5K+ reviews
- Private label manufacturers with economies of scale
- Chinese sellers with 30-50% cost advantage
- Price range: $7.99-24.99, incredibly compressed

### Month 3
Realized: the 150,000 monthly searches were spread across 12,000 different products. Per-product search volume: 12.5 searches/month per listing. She'd need #1-5 ranking to get 100+ monthly searches, not the bottom 100.

Increased ad spend to $1,200. Results: 65 sales, $1,560 revenue. But ad cost $0.18 per sale = 10% of revenue. Combined with FBA fees (25%), profit margin became negative.

### Month 4
Cut ad spend back to $600. Organic sales collapsed: 20 units/month. Inventory: 460 units unsold. Storage fees piling up. Market was simply too competitive for a unknown seller with no reviews or brand credibility.

She pivoted: tried selling screen protectors (different product, same category hell). Same problem. Eventually, quit after month 5.

## Key Failure Mechanism

**Market Selection Failure:** Rachel chose phone cases because of search volume (150K/month) without understanding that this volume is spread across 12,000 listings. The actual addressable market for a new, unreviewed seller is <50/month—insufficient to generate profit. Her influencer audience (45K) couldn't penetrate Amazon's algorithm-driven discovery; they preferred social commerce or expected discounts.

## What They Wish They Knew

1. **High search volume doesn't mean low competition:** Phone cases: 150K searches, 12,000 sellers = ~12.5 searches/product. Not viable
2. **Amazon is algorithm-driven; social audience doesn't transfer:** Her 45K followers are useless on Amazon; algorithm ranks by reviews and conversion rate, not social proof
3. **Influencer margins don't apply to Amazon:** She's used to 3-5% conversion rates on Instagram. Amazon conversion rates: 0.5-1% for unknown sellers
4. **Saturated categories have 3+ of these symptoms:** 5K+ established listings, $10-20 price range, Chinese dominance, <5 searches/product for new sellers

## The Math
- Revenue (5 months): $4,200
- COGS: $1,100
- FBA fees and referral: $1,050
- Ad spend: $3,600
- Storage and misc.: $250
- Inventory liquidation loss (40% clearance): $900
- Time (150 hours @ $18/hr opportunity): $2,700
- Total loss: **-$2,600**
- Effective hourly rate: **-$17.33/hour**

**Lesson:** "Having an audience felt like a business moat. It wasn't. Amazon doesn't care about my Instagram followers. It cares about reviews, conversion rate, and algorithm ranking. I chose a category with 12,000 competitors, thinking 150K searches meant opportunity. I didn't understand that search volume per seller was actually less than 20/month. Classic beginner mistake."

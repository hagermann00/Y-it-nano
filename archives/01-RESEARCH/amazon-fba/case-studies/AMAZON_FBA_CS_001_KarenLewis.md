# Case Study 001: Karen Lewis

## Character Profile
- **Name:** Karen Lewis (composite, anonymized)
- **Age:** 34
- **Background:** HR manager at mid-size company
- **Income:** $75,000/year
- **Why They Tried:** Wanted to build "passive income" business; watched 15+ YouTube videos claiming "$5K/month possible"

## Their Plan
- Sell kitchen gadgets (peelers, graters, microplane sets)
- Initial investment: $4,000 for 500 units
- Expected: 60 units/month = 8-month inventory burn
- Projected profit: $8-10 per unit × 60/month = $480-600/month within 3 months
- Strategy: Private label on AliExpress, optimize listings, grow via reviews

## What Happened

### Months 1-2
Received inventory in September. Listed products, ran promotions. Only 18 units sold Month 1, 22 units Month 2. Reality shock: demand was 1/3 of forecast. By November, had 440 unsold units in FBA warehouse. Storage fees eating $80-120/month. Karen doubled ad spend trying to move inventory.

### Months 3-4
Margin catastrophe discovered: Referral fees (15%) + FBA fulfillment ($2.50/unit) + storage costs ($0.30/unit averaged) = $4.20 per unit. After COGS ($3.50), profit was only $0.80/unit instead of projected $8. To break even, needed 400+ sales just to cover storage and fees.

### Month 5 (The Kill Shot)
February 15 hit. Long-term storage fee: $1,800 (90% of inventory now >180 days old). Combined with accumulated losses, Karen had spent $5,200 with only $900 in revenue. Couldn't afford to restock or continue. Closed business mid-March.

## Key Failure Mechanism

**Inventory Overstock + Storage Fee Cascade:** Karen's $4,000 inventory investment was mathematically sound IF she moved 60 units/month. She moved 18-22. The slow-moving inventory triggered storage fees that compounded losses monthly, then catastrophically on the Feb 15 assessment date. Lost 83% of investment.

## What They Wish They Knew

1. **Demand forecasting is 80% of success:** Using guru formulas (search volume × conversion rate) overstated demand 3-4x
2. **Storage costs turn inventory into liability:** After 90 days, inventory becomes negative-ROI
3. **Long-term storage fees are business killers:** That single Feb 15 fee ($1,800) wiped out 18 months of projected profit
4. **Private label requires product-market validation FIRST:** Should have tested 50 units, not 500

## Timeline to Loss
- Sept: -$4,000 inventory + $200 FBA fees
- Oct: -$80 storage
- Nov: -$100 storage, -$500 ads
- Dec: -$120 storage
- Jan: -$120 storage
- Feb: -$1,800 long-term storage fee = **Total: -$5,200**

**Effective Hourly Rate:** -$28.89/hour

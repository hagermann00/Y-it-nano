# Case Study 004: Jefferson Reid

## Character Profile
- **Name:** Jefferson Reid (composite, anonymized)
- **Age:** 38
- **Background:** Copywriter, freelancer
- **Income:** $55,000/year (inconsistent)
- **Why They Tried:** "Amazon is stable. Policies won't change much. Passive income I can count on."

## Their Plan
- Sell home organization products (drawer organizers, shelf dividers, wall mounts)
- Initial investment: $2,500 for 250 units
- Calculated sustainable margins: 20% (conservative, he thought)
- Target: $400-500/month profit by month 4
- Timeline: 12-month runway to profitability

## What Happened

### January-March 2024
Launched products in January 2024. By March, had sold 180 units over 3 months. Profit margin: roughly 20% as calculated. Monthly revenue: $900-1,100. Jeff felt on track. Profitability seemed achievable by month 5-6.

### April 1, 2024: POLICY CHANGE
Amazon announced FBA fee increase, effective April 1. Standard-size items: +15-20% across most categories. Oversized items: +25%. Jeff's products were small-to-medium standard size.

**Before April 1:** FBA fulfillment fee: $2.50/unit
**After April 1:** FBA fulfillment fee: $2.95-3.15/unit

Gross impact on his product line: 15% increase in fulfillment costs. His 20% margin instantly became 15%. Not catastrophic—but it compressed profit targets significantly.

### April-June 2024
Sales continued at 60 units/month. Profit decreased from $200-250/month to $155-180/month. Still viable. But...

### June 1, 2024: NEW FEES
Amazon introduced "Returns Processing Fees" for high-return categories. Jeff's category had baseline 12% return rate, triggering new 3% revenue fee.

**New fee structure:**
- Referral (15%): $1.80
- FBA fulfillment (new rate): $2.95
- Returns processing (new): $0.36
- Storage: $0.15
- Total: $5.26 per $12 sale

His margin compressed from 15% to 8%. Now barely profitable. Monthly profit: $60-80.

### August 2024
Inventory aging prompted increased storage assessments. FBA Capacity Utilization Surcharge triggered (new 2024 policy): additional 0.24/unit if inventory sitting >22 weeks.

Jeff realized his "conservative 20% margin" calculation from January was now 8% by August. Over 8 months, his business had 60% margin compression due to policy changes ONLY. His sales and efficiency hadn't changed.

### September 2024
Jeff evaluated: $60-80/month profit ($12-16/hour rate) while managing inventory. Remaining inventory: 70 units. Rather than fight compressing margins, he liquidated at 30% discount and closed.

## Key Failure Mechanism

**Amazon Policy Changes + Fee Increases:** Jeff's failure wasn't due to poor execution or market selection. His products sold well, margins were calculated conservatively. But Amazon's policy and fee changes (3 separate events in 6 months) compressed his margins from 20% → 15% → 8% → effectively unviable. He couldn't adapt fast enough because he was already operating at razor-thin margins.

## What They Wish They Knew

1. **Amazon fees change constantly and are non-negotiable:** April 2023 (20-30% increase), April 2024 (15-25% increase), June 2024 (new fee categories), October 2024 (reimbursement window collapse)
2. **"Conservative 20% margins" aren't conservative in FBA:** Policy changes alone can erase 40-60% of projected profits
3. **Business model requires 25%+ margins to survive:** To absorb 4-5 policy changes/fee increases across 3-5 years
4. **Passive income requires active monitoring:** Policy changes require immediate pivots or liquidation

## The Math
- Revenue (9 months): $5,400
- Gross profit: $864
- Fulfillment and admin time: 160 hours × $15/hr = $2,400
- Net loss: **-$1,536**
- Inventory liquidation loss: $840
- Total loss: **-$2,400**
- Effective hourly rate: **-$15/hour**

**Lesson:** "Amazon didn't change its business in a day. But over 6 months, the fees and policies shifted so much that my margin went from 20% to 8%. I watched it happen monthly but couldn't stop it. Turns out 'passive income' requires obsessively monitoring a platform's policy changes."
